Himachal Pradesh features numerous revered temples like the famous Naina Devi, Jwalaji, and Chamunda Devi shrines, the ancient Masroor Rock-cut Temples, the Shree Bhimakali Temple, and the popular Jakhoo Temple in Shimla. The region is a major Hindu pilgrimage destination, with many ancient sites dedicated to Hindu deities, including various Shakti Peethas, such as the Chintpurni, Brajeshwari, and Kangra Devi temples. Major Temples & Shrines Shree Naina Devi Temple: One of the important Shakti Peethas, located on a hilltop. Shree Jwalaji Temple: Famous for its naturally occurring flames, a significant Hindu pilgrimage site. Shree Chamunda Devi Temple: Dedicated to Goddess Chamunda, it's another key site in the region. Masroor Rock-cut Temples: An ancient architectural marvel, these are temples carved directly into rock faces. Shree Bhimakali Temple: A prominent temple known for its unique architectural style. Jakhoo Temple: Located in Shimla, it is dedicated to the Hindu deity Hanuman. Shakti Peethas Himachal Pradesh is home to several significant Shakti Peethas, which are holy sites dedicated to the goddess Shakti or Durga. Key Shakti Peethas include: Naina Devi, Jwalaji, Chintpurni Devi, Brajeshwari Devi, and Chamunda Devi. Other Notable Temples Lakshana Devi Temple: An ancient temple located in Bharmour, known for its historic significance. Baba Balak Nath Temple, Deotsidh: A revered pilgrimage site dedicated to Baba Balak Nath. Tara Devi Temple: Another popular temple in the Shimla hills.
